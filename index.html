Welcome to the Singh Quantum Lab

Based at Guru Nanak Dev University in Amritsar, our research group is at the forefront of quantum information science. We focus on developing novel quantum algorithms, designing fault-tolerant quantum computing architectures, and exploring the fundamental nature of quantum entanglement. Our mission is to contribute to India's growing role in the global quantum technology landscape by conducting world-class research and training the next generation of quantum scientists and engineers.

We employ a blend of theoretical physics and computational science to tackle complex problems in quantum simulation and cryptography. The core values of our lab are rigor, creativity, and a collaborative spirit in solving some of the most profound scientific challenges. We believe that a strong, interdisciplinary approach is key to unlocking the full potential of quantum mechanics. We invite you to explore our website to learn more about our work on superconducting qubits, quantum machine learning, and secure quantum communication.

Research Projects

Superconducting Qubit Architectures

Our primary focus is on the design, simulation, and fabrication of next-generation superconducting qubits. We explore novel circuit designs to enhance coherence times, reduce gate errors, and improve qubit connectivity. By minimizing environmental noise and crosstalk, we aim to build more robust and scalable quantum processors suitable for fault-tolerant quantum computation. This research involves extensive use of microwave simulation software and theoretical modeling of quantum circuits.

Current investigations include the development of transmons with tunable couplers and the integration of 3D-cavity architectures to achieve higher fidelity operations. We collaborate closely with fabrication facilities to translate our theoretical designs into physical devices for experimental validation.

Quantum Machine Learning Algorithms

This research area explores the intersection of quantum computing and artificial intelligence. We develop and analyze quantum algorithms that can offer significant speedups for machine learning tasks. Our work includes creating quantum kernels for support vector machines, designing quantum neural networks, and exploring variational quantum algorithms for optimization problems.

We are particularly interested in applying these algorithms to real-world datasets in fields such as drug discovery and financial modeling. A key challenge we address is the development of noise-resilient algorithms that can run effectively on today's noisy intermediate-scale quantum (NISQ) devices.

Fault-Tolerant Quantum Error Correction

The fragility of quantum states is a major obstacle to building large-scale quantum computers. Our group works on the theory and implementation of quantum error correction (QEC) codes. We design codes that can protect quantum information from decoherence and other errors by encoding it across multiple physical qubits.

We are currently focused on surface codes and color codes, analyzing their performance thresholds and resource requirements. Our research also involves developing efficient decoding algorithms that can identify and correct errors in real-time, a crucial step towards achieving fault-tolerant quantum computation.
